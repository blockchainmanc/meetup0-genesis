WTF is Blockchain?

"Blockchain is a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptography"

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockchain

so WTF is Blockchain?

The typical analogy

  • A Blockchain behaves like a bank ledger recording a stream of transactions
  • Cryptography is layered on top to secure and validate a Blockchain

Some facts

Decentralised

  • Fault tolerant - decentralisation & peer-to-peer node topology
  • Collaboration and consensus algorithms
  • Global networks can bypass political and economic problems
  • Potential for radicalising socioeconomics

Immutability

  • Blockchain's are typically immutable
  • A decision taken by a chains creators - corrections are possible but heavily criticised
Whats the point of Blockchain that's mutable, just use DB?

A purist view of immutability

  • Purist believe Blockchain's should be Immutable
  • In reality this should be based on requirements and needs e.g. private Blockchain may require corrections
Once a Blockchain transaction has received a sufficient level of validation, cryptography ensures that it can never be changed

Most are public, accountable and trustless in nature

  • Publicly available for interrogation
  • Accountable as transactions can be traced
  • Trustless - provided by cryptography & censuses, not an organisation
Created by consensus between chain participants - `proof-of-work` & `proof-of-stake`

Programmable smart contracts Ethereum, Neo, Qtum

  • Applications utilising network features
  • No single owner of a solution, the contract lives on the chain and the chain is owned by all nodes
  • Chain participant's execute and run smart contracts

Distributed ledger technology (DLT) Distributed Blockchain ledger (DBL)

A distributed ledger is a database that can securely record financial, physical or electronic assets for sharing across a network through entirely transparent updates of information. Distributed ledger technology could provide government with new tools to reduce fraud, error and the cost of paper intensive processes. It also has the potential to provide new ways of assuring ownership and provenance for goods and intellectual property.

- https://www.gov.uk/government/news/distributed-ledger-technology-beyond-block-chain

To summarise

Blockchain's can be distrusted, public, peer-to-peer, fault tolerant, programmable distrusted ledgers, with automated consensus and accountability at its core!
  • True power lies in DLT & consensus problem solving a Blockchain network can introduce
  • Removing the need for centralised organisations who enforced trust

Who created it?

  • Theoretical Blockchain papers went back to the early 90's
  • Bitcoin the first public decentralised Blockchain created in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto

What was Bitcoin's goal?

  • Decentralisation & Openness
  • Often referred to as foundation technology which will facility new disruptive technology changes
  • New transaction types and assets types
  • Disrupt traditional global economic and social systems
  • Remove the reliance on existing centralised banking systems

Real world examples

  • Bank sectors - payments, clearing, escrow, stocks and share management
  • Supply chain management/distribution - raw materials e.g. Diamonds
  • IoT device management and network security
  • Legal services - writing the law into the Blockchain
  • Land, equity and digital asset management
  • Gambling - payouts, bets, resulting
  • Loyalty and reward mechanisms
  • Identity stores - universal identity and health care records

Things a Blockchain isn't

  • The answer to life, the universe and everything
  • Something which will replace all traditional databases solutions
  • Implementation for all use-cases
  • Somewhere to store of large amounts data (yet...)
  • About making a quick buck

What is a Smart Contract?

  • Code that is deployed and executed on a Blockchain

EVM - Ethereum Virtual Machine

  • Limited capabilities
  • Relatively early stage of development
  • Other Blockchains are following in the footsteps e.g. Qtum, NEO
