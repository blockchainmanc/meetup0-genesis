WTF ... Blockchain?

"Blockchain is a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptography"

- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockchain

... so WTF is Blockchain?

Hit me with some facts

  • A Blockchain typically behaves like a bank ledger, recording a stream of events/transactions
  • Cryptography is heavily utilised to build and secure a Blockchain

Typically decentralised & peer-to-peer

  • Fault tolerant due to this decentralisation & peer-to-peer nature
  • Peer-to-peer collaboration and consensus based algorithms can do utilised
  • Global networks can bypass local political and economic problems
  • Potential for radicalising socioeconomics

Immutablity

  • Often Blockchain's are immutable but this is not always the case
  • A decision taken by a chains creators - corrections are possible but heavily criticised
Whats the point of Blockchain that's mutable, just use DB?

A purist view of immutablity

  • A purist perspective is that a Blockchain should be Immutable
  • In reality this should be based on requirements and needs e.g. private blockchain may require corrections
Once a Blockchain transaction has received a sufficient level of validation, some cryptography ensures that it can never be changed

Most are public, accountable and trustless in nature

Created by a consensus between the chain participants - `proof-of-work` & `proof-of-stake`
  • proof-of-work - Bitcoin
  • proof-of-stack - Ethereum (in the future)

Many are programmable via smart contracts

  • Applications utilising the above features
  • e.g. Ethereum, Neo, Quantum

To summarise

So its a global public peer-to-peer, fault tolerant, programmable distrusted ledger, typically with consensus validation and accountability at its core!
