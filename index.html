WTF ... Blockchain?

"Blockchain is a continuously growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked and secured using cryptography"

- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockchain

... so WTF is Blockchain?

Some facts

  • A Blockchain behaves like a bank ledger, recording a stream of events/transactions
  • Cryptography is utilised to build, secure and validate a Blockchain

Decentralised

  • Fault tolerant due to this decentralisation & peer-to-peer nature
  • Automated collaboration and consensus based algorithms can do utilised
  • Global networks can bypass local political and economic problems
  • Potential for radicalising socioeconomics

Immutability

  • Often Blockchain's are immutable but this is not always the case
  • A decision taken by a chains creators - corrections are possible but heavily criticised
Whats the point of Blockchain that's mutable, just use DB?

A purist view of immutability

  • A purist perspective is that a Blockchain should be Immutable
  • In reality this should be based on requirements and needs e.g. private blockchain may require corrections
Once a Blockchain transaction has received a sufficient level of validation, cryptography ensures that it can never be changed

Most are public, accountable and trustless in nature

  • Publicly available for integration
  • Accountable as transactions can be chased
  • Trustless as cryptography & censuses provides validation, not an organisation
Created by consensus (algorithms) between chain participants - `proof-of-work` (POW) & `proof-of-stake` (POS)

Programmable smart contracts Ethereum, Neo, Qtum

  • Applications utilising network features
  • No single owner of a problem
  • Chain participant's execute and run smart contracts

To summarise

Blockchain is a global public peer-to-peer, fault tolerant, programmable distrusted ledger, typically with consensus validation and accountability at its core!
  • True power lies consensus validation and communal problem solving a blockchain network can introduce
  • Removal of centralised organisations who enforced trust

Who created it?

  • Theoretical Blockchain papers went back to the early 90's
  • Bitcoin the first public decentralised Blockchain created in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto
  • Released just as the financial crash hits

What was its goal?

  • Decentralisation & Openness
  • Disrupt traditional global economic and social systems
  • Often referred to as foundation technology which will facility new disruptive technology changes
  • One of Bitcoin's goals are to remove the reliance on existing centralised banking systems

Real world examples

  • Bank sectors - payments, clearing, escrow, share & dividend management
  • Supply chain management/distribution
  • IOT device management and network security
  • Legal services - writing the law to the blockchain
  • Land, equity and digital asset management
  • Gambling - payouts, bets, resulting
  • Loyalty and reward mechanisms
  • Identity stores - universal identity and health care records

Things a Blockchain isn't

  • The answer to life, the universe and everything
  • Something which replace traditional sql/nosql databases
  • Something which all use-cases will require
  • A store of large amounts data
